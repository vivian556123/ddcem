Denoising Diffusion Conditional Model for Target Speech Extraction

Abstract

Target Speech Extraction (TSE) is a crucial task in the field of speech processing, focusing on isolating the clean speech of a specific speaker from complex mixtures. Although discriminative methods dominate the TSE task, they can introduce some distortion to the speech perception quality. Conversely, generative approaches, especially diffusion-based methods, enhance speech quality but lead to unsatisfied inference speed. To resolve this contradiction, we propose Denoising Diffusion Conditional Expectation Model (DDCEM), an efficient generative approach for TSE that accommodates multi- and single-speaker, noisy and clean scenarios. More practically, we propose Regenerate-DDCEM (R-DDCEM), a variant of DDCEM's inference process, which can regenerate and optimize speech quality given pre-processed speech by a discriminative model. Our method outperforms conventional methods across all metrics and exhibits notable strengths in inference efficiency and robustness of unseen tasks.
